Famous mathematicians and their contributions

Leonhard Euler (1707 - 1783)

Nationality: Swiss

Major Contributions:
  • Euler's identity
  • Graph theory
  • Number theory
  • Calculus of variations
  • Topology
Biography:

One of the most prolific mathematicians in history, Euler made groundbreaking contributions to nearly every area of mathematics. He introduced much of the mathematical notation still used today and solved many important problems in analysis, number theory, and mechanics.

Isaac Newton (1643 - 1727)

Nationality: English

Major Contributions:
  • Calculus
  • Laws of motion
  • Universal gravitation
  • Binomial theorem
  • Method of fluxions
Biography:

Newton revolutionized mathematics and physics with his development of calculus and laws of motion. His work laid the foundation for classical mechanics and much of modern mathematics.

Carl Friedrich Gauss (1777 - 1855)

Nationality: German

Major Contributions:
  • Number theory
  • Differential geometry
  • Statistics
  • Analysis
  • Astronomy
Biography:

Known as the 'Prince of Mathematics', Gauss made significant contributions to many fields including number theory, statistics, analysis, differential geometry, geodesy, geophysics, mechanics, electrostatics, astronomy, matrix theory, and optics.
